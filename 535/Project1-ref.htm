Project I – Tool Research & Demonstration

Objective:

To learn what tools are available to aid in the development and testing of software, beyond the typical compilers, debuggers, etc.

Pre-Work:

You must prepare a brief paragraph stating what tool you wish to research and present in class, email it to the instructor, and get approval PRIOR to starting this part of the project.

Description:

There are many tools that are designed to help with some of the tasks typically encountered during software development. Many of these tools are familiar, such as compilers, linkers, debuggers, etc. Some are less familiar, but can be invaluable during development if used properly.

 

Examples of these tools include:

1.                 Lint

2.                 Make

3.                 Interface builders

4.                 Class wizards

5.                 Class browsers

6.                 Coverage analyzers

7.                 Automated testing tools

Output:

A presentation of the tool in terms of

1.                 Purpose (i.e., what is it intended for?)

2.                 Capabilities (i.e., what can it do?)

3.                 Pros and Cons

 

You will be asked to share your research with the class in Module 3/Session 10 as part of the discussions.
